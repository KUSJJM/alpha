Lectures

Lecture 1 - Structure of the Module

Presentation given in the first lecture explaining how the course will be organised. Much of this information such as timetable, assessment strategy, and text books is in the Module Guide.


Lecture 2 - Introduction to Network Concepts

"Introduction to key networking concepts of packetization, encapsulation and protocol layering. A review of OSI Reference Model and responsibilities and issues of each layer.


Lecture 3 - Networking and IP Addressing

Introduction to IPv4 addressing, routing and address resolution (ARP). Includes the design methodology used to develop an (fixed-length mask) IP addressing scheme.


Lecture 4 - Network Technologies

This part of the module covers the characteristics of physical communication media (copper, fibre, air) together with LAN and WAN technologies and wireless. (There will be some online quizzes to support this material - I will try to post the first one on Monday.)


Lecture 5 - Reliability and Performance

Key reliability mechanisms in TCP/IP including ports, segmentation, PAR and connection-oriented vs. connection-less delivery. Understanding congestion issues and the TCP congestion control algorithm.
